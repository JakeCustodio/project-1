Grand Tetons

Grand Teton National Park is a national park of the United States in northwestern Wyoming. At approximately 310,000 acres (1,300 km2), the park includes the major peaks of the 40-mile-long (64 km) Teton Range as well as most of the northern sections of the valley known as Jackson Hole. Grand Teton National Park is 10 miles (16 km) south of Yellowstone National Park, to which it is connected by the John D. Rockefeller Jr. Memorial Parkway which is managed by the National Park Service. Along with surrounding national forests, these three protected areas constitute the almost 22-million-acre (89,000-square-kilometer) Greater Yellowstone Ecosystem, one of the world's largest intact mid-latitude temperate ecosystems.

A Mystery in Stone

John Colter was a well-known trapper and mountain man, and may have been the first white explorer of the greater Yellowstone area. A previous member of Lewis and Clark's expeditions, Colter's exploration of the area served as the basis of maps of the Teton Range and Yellowstone River, but his exact route remains up for debate. A crucial piece of evidence– a stone with Colter's name and the date 1808 engraved into it, found in what is now Eastern Idaho– would clear up much of the ambiguity, if it could only be authenticated.
