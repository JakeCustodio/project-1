Natural Wonders

Pythagoras and his followers existed at a time when there was much less of a distinction between philosophy as we understand it today, and more mechanical academic fields such as mathematics, music and physics. Pythagoras himself was a prolific mathematician and is also remembered for the formula which carries his name. His mathematical acumen provided the basis for his philosophical and moral beliefs, equating the principles of justice to the principles of geometric proportion.

Pythagoreans believed strongly in the goodness of the natural order, and the injustice that came from transgressing it. Justice, then, exists both as a natural and social concept, and results from when all elements of a system receive their due, in accordance with the natural order.
